Three misconceptions about Computer Science:

  1. Computer Science is the study of computers.
  2. Computer science is the study of how to write computer programs.
  3. Computer science is the study of the uses and applications of computers and software.

Briefly define:

  1. Computer Science: The study of the principles and use of computers.
  2. algorithm: A procedure for solving a mathematical problem in a finite number of steps that frequently involves repetition of an operation; broadly; a step-by-step method for accomplishing some task.
  3. sequential operation: A sequential instruction carries out a single well defined task.When the task is finished, the algorithm moves on to the next operation.
  4. conditional operation: They ask the question, and the next operation is then selectedon the basis of the answer to that question.
  5. iterative operation: These are the looping instructions. They tell us not to go on to the next instruction but, instead, to go back and repeat the execution of a previous block of instructions.
  6. stored program computer: A computer that stores instructions in its memory to enable it to perform a variety of tasks in sequence or intermittently.
  7. programmer: Writes commands in a high-level language.
  8. integrated circuit: integrated circuits with transistors, resistors, and capacitors were photographicallly etched onto a piece of silicon, which further reduced the size and cost of computers.

Briefly describe these computer generations:

  1. First generation computer: The first appearance of UNIVAC I and the IBM 701, the first computer built by the company that would soon become a leader in this new field. They were used only by trained personnel and only in specialized locations.
  2. Second generation computer: A step up in computers with a major change in size and complexity. Increase in reliability and reduced costs. They were ttainable by medium-sized businesses, colleges, and government agencies. This was also the era of the appearance of FORTRAN and COBOL.
  3. Third generation computer: lasted from about 1965-1975. This era introduced the intergrated circuit which further reduced the size and cost of computers. It also saw the birth of the sofware industry such as accounting packages and statistical programs. By this time, they were no longer a rarity.
  4. Fourth generation computer: roughly from 1975-1985. This era saw the appearance of the first microcomputer. More software was developed, electronic mail is now being used, and graphical user interfaces are available. Computers are being used in cars and household appliances.
  5. Fifth generation computer: We are currently in this generation. So much is changing so fast that the concept of distinct generations of computer development has outlived its usefulness. A couple developments are:
    • Ultra-high-resolution graphics for 3D imaging, animatin, movie making, video games, and virtual riality.
    • integrated digital devices incorporating data, television, telephone, camera, fax, the internet, and medical monitoring.

FORTRAN:

  1. What does the acronym FORTRAN stand for? - Formula Translation
  2. When was FORTRAN developed?- Created in 1954. Commercial release in 1957.
  3. What was it used for? - Fortran made code comprehensible to people with expertise in fields other than computing, opening programming to mathematicians and scientists.
  4. Is FORTRAN still being used today? If so, what for? Yes. Doppler radar weather forecasts or atmospheric and oceanic studies, as well as simulating nanoparticles, genomes, DNA and atomic structures.

COBOL:

  1. What does the acronym COBOL stand for? - Common Business Oriented Language
  2. When was COBOL developed? - 1959
  3. What was it used for? - Common Business-Oriented Language (COBOL) is a programming language similar to English that is widely used to develop business-oriented applications in the area of commercial data processing.
  4. Is COBOL still being used today? If so, what for? - business, finance, and administrative systems for companies and governments.
